I study how AI and embodied systems shape human reasoning, learning, and action.

My research asks when computational assistance extends a person’s capabilities and when it begins to obscure judgment, authorship, or skill.

I use qualitative research to frame those tensions, then build interaction and physical prototypes that make them possible to study in use.

Human AI interaction · human augmentation · embodied systems · digital health

Selected research systems
image · Gyan AI
01

Gyan AI

An AI clinical reasoning simulator that asks learners to commit to an interpretation before receiving model feedback. Three iterations explored how the timing and modality of assistance could support reasoning without bypassing it.

RoleResearch, interaction framing, prototype design Research focusLearning, reflection, confidence calibration ContextUW Dempsey, top 16 of 174 Year2024
Design insightPlacing a reflection step before model feedback positions the AI as a critique partner rather than a source of completion.
image · SoleSoothe
02

SoleSoothe

A sensing insole concept for chronic pain that maps pressure at reported pain points and connects those signals to targeted relief. The body, rather than a screen, is the primary site of interaction.

SystemWearable sensing and responsive relief Research focusOn body feedback, legibility, immediate response ContextIndustrial design studio
Design propositionLocating sensing and response on the body can make the feedback loop more immediate and interpretable than screen mediated control alone.
image · Juno Care
03

Juno Care

A postpartum recovery platform developed with an anesthesiologist at UW Medicine. The project maps fragmented care across time and treats recovery as a changing condition rather than a fixed sequence of touchpoints.

RoleDesign research, systems design Research focusLongitudinal support, care transitions, uncertainty ContextUW Social Entrepreneurship Fellowship
Design propositionDesigning around moments of instability, rather than an average recovery journey, can produce more credible support.

Research questions
01

Assistance and reasoning

How can an AI system improve performance while preserving reflection, confidence calibration, and ownership of the underlying reasoning?

02

Embodied feedback

How can sensing and response remain understandable when computation moves away from a screen and closer to the body?

03

Support over time

How should systems adapt to changing human needs without flattening uncertainty or taking control away from the person?

Methods in practiceInterviews, qualitative synthesis, precedent review, systems mapping, interaction prototyping, physical prototyping, and iterative evaluation.
